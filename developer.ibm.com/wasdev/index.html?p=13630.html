Celebrating Java EE 7 in WAS Liberty


Laura Cowen
Published on July 9, 2015 / Updated on August 21, 2015
1 Comments

Yesterday, the Hursley part of the WAS Liberty team celebrated our Java EE 7 release of Liberty with a BBQ (on the first rainy day of the recent summery stretch but, hey, we’re British). It involved food, fun, and cake. Meanwhile, in dev teams elsewhere in the world (the sun never sets on Liberty development), more cake was had.

Here are the BBQ cooks (Nikki (Hursley 2nd Line Manager), Kevin (Test Architect), Mike (DevOps Tester), and Dave (QA Developer):


And the optional, more exotic BBQ offerings:


Fun

Nikki had a go at the water balloon catapult (with Kate (Developer) and Jeremy (Architect) obliging as ballast):


Part-way through the afternoon, Joe (Developer) produced an ‘engineering challenge’ for anyone who wanted to take part; teams had to safely deliver an egg from >4m high in the air to the ground. With the aid of this contraption (that’s Joe on the right; Neil (Developer) was employed to give extra height and stability):


All, except the teams who cheated (eg by boiling the egg; curiously, those teams were the ones that each included a manager), completed the challenge successfully. So the winning team was the one who’d done it the quickest, which was Tim (Developer and Iain (Developer):


But Holly (Developer) and Ross (Developer) got special mention for the aesthetics of their parachute-inspired solution:


Our senior technical peeps, Graham (Architect) and Jeremy, occupied themselves building rockets to launch at the trees:
